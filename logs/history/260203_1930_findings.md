# Highlights: Laboratory Pages Implementation

**Date**: 2026-02-03 19:30
**Context**: Update 3 - Creating individual pages for participating laboratories

---

## Issue Identified

Laboratory information was scattered across journals and `[[Laboratorios]]` MOC without individual pages. This made it difficult to:
- Track detailed information per laboratory
- Maintain historical participation by round
- Document equipment inventory
- Manage individual laboratory status

---

## Solution Implemented

**Decision**: Each participating laboratory must have its own individual page with standard structure.

**Justification**:
- Granular tracking: participation by round, equipment details, observations
- Linkable: can reference from journals, rounds, and other pages
- Scalable: new laboratories can be added consistently
- Historical: maintains record of past and future participation

## Pattern Established

**Rule**: Every participating laboratory must have its own page in `/pages/` with the standard structure.

**MOC Role**: `[[Laboratorios]]` acts as:
1. Registry of all labs (by status)
2. Template reference for new lab pages
3. Quick access point to individual lab pages

**Linking Strategy**:
- Journals use wiki links when mentioning labs: `[[SIATA]]`, `[[Universidad de Medellín]]`
- `[[Prueba Piloto]]` links to lab pages for confirmations
- Individual lab pages link back to `[[Prueba Piloto]]` for round participation
